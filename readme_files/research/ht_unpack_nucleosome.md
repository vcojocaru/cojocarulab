<div style="text-align: justify;">
<p>
    This project is focused on discovering the mechanisms by which a special class of proteins known as
    pioneer transcription factors bind to and open pieces of genomic DNA. The longer term goal will be to
    redesign these proteins to improve theit capacity of opening genomic DNA. Why focusing on these
    proteins ? Because pioneer factors (Zaret & Mango, 2016) are capable of inducing conversions
    between different cell types which have the potential to be used in regenerative therapies (Chambers et
    al, 2011, Xu et al., 2015, Lin et al. 2018, Wang et al., 2021).However, such conversions have a low
    efficiency and are not controllable. Therefore, revealing the detailed mechanisms by which pioneer
    factors recognize and unravel genomic DNA will provide means to redesign these proteins to optimize
    and control cellular conversions for their more efficient application in regenerative therapies. </p>
