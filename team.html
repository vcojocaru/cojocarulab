Vlad Cojocaru

Group Coordinator

Principal researcher with expertise in computational molecular biochemistry. I have extensive interdisciplinary experience and a background in physics, chemistry, and biology. Throughout my scientific career, progressing from a doctoral candidate to leading a research group, I have strived to uncover how the three-dimensional structure and dynamics of molecules influence their function within cells. I have discovered how proteins impact the folding of RNA molecules, how drug-metabolizing enzymes embed themselves in lipid membranes and channel drug molecules to and from their active sites, and how specialized proteins that regulate gene expression bind to and explore DNA. For these discoveries, I have employed various molecular modeling and simulation methods, as well as self-developed or established analysis tools. I have always been ambitious and enjoyed challenges, particularly in designing and leading projects within interdisciplinary teams. Most recently, I led a small research team and guided students to successfully complete internships or doctoral programs. I have published highly regarded collaborative research articles in prestigious journals, delivered lectures at renowned international conferences, and secured funding from multiple sources for my ongoing research endeavors.

Roxana Vasarhelyi

Phd student

RNA recognition motifs (RRMs) are among the main RNA binding motifs found in proteins, also known as the ribonucleoprotein (RNP) motif or RNP-type RNA-binding domain (RBD). RRMs are present in a considerable number of proteins involved in nearly all facets of RNA processing and transport.(1) Furthermore RNA-binding proteins have been linked to abnormal expression, malfunction, and aggregation in several main types of human diseases, including cancer, neurological disorders, and muscle atrophies.(2)
